Descending to the shortcut, you remember that these are very unfriendly looking trees - they have dark scars on their bark that look like eyes looking back at you, and ever-branching, dendritic limbs that seem to tessellate on and on forever. They are so densely-packed that, even without any foliage, they blot out the sun. You are reminded of the horrible man-eating spiders crawling around in the literary Mirkwood…

Then again, perhaps the trees aren’t so bad after all. With the gentle snowfall landing on the fingerlike branches, they appear to be penciled into a drawing.

The crunching of Zimmer’s shoes ceases as he stops suddenly, and gestures to you to do the same. Through the disturbances of a slight wind, you strain your hearing, but then…
