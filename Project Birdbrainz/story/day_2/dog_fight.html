It is difficult to see between the tall organisms, be they plant or fungus, especially around the dense center.

As you get closer, you realize that the treelike things form walls, like in a maze, but less extreme. They appear to spiral around the center in tighter and tighter loops as they approach the middle.

"Into the woods…" Ari says, unenthused by the cramped space.

After walking around blind for a few moments, you get to the center: here is a grassy floor growing with vibrant brilliance, even flowers, in the middle of winter! But here, too, was something else - a small stone with the name "Napoleon" painted onto it in gold.

Zimmer is rubbing his eyes, "This is too much… of all creatures!". Ari, too, appears to have tears welling in their own. It's a beautiful sight, in an uncanny sort of way.

BOOM. BOOM.

The sound of thuds under the ground as it shakes adds to the distress of your friends, and puts fear in your heart.

One low groan. Then a higher pitch stretching, and stretching. POP. The loose dirt begins to fall into the hole, and an eyeless English bulldog crawls its way out, dead and covered in fungus.


Wait! Don't kill it!

Kill it with fire!
