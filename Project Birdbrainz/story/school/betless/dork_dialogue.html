”My friend - I may lack common sense” he admits “but I have some self-awareness… but only a little. But still, I can’t be the only one…”.

The woodshop is in a little outbuilding that you have to walk quite a ways to get to. The only windows interrupting the grey bricks of the exterior are too high for anyone to look out of, and probably just there for natural lighting. Behind the building is a small gravel patch, where a few large piles of sawdust straddle the edge of the vast forest that lay beyond. There’s also a little garden box and a compost pile, a pet project of Mr. Walsh’s. The small lot extends quite a ways back, enshrined by maples and oaks whose fingerlike appendages are, at this time, barren.

“Right this way,” Zimmer says, as you stand before another set of blue double-doors, this one a little more weather-worn and rust-covered.
